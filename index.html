       My life always has been a series of challenges. During my high school years, I always had a dream of studying abroad. In 2009, I transferred from Hongchun High School in Korea to Oak Grove School in Ojai, California, the United States as Junior. To do so, I had to learn English almost from scratch to fulfill my dream. In 2012, I was admitted to the University of California, Riverside as a Mechanical Engineering major. After a few years of learning, it came to a realization that I had no passion for engineering but I was just following my parents' wishes.

      Therefore, I decided to change my path and got myself into the music department. After 2 years of learning music theory and composition from scratch, I went back to Korea and got myself ready for my mandatory military service. In the meantime, I learned digital music production from Record Factory in Seoul and received a certification. After serving the mandatory military service in Korea for about 21 months. For the service, I applied for KATUSA (Korean Augmentation Troops to the United States Army) program and got admitted into the program. I learned the abilities and mindsets of being a successful leader in the United States Army stationed in Camp Humphrey's, Pyeongtek, South Korea. I have graduated from the University of California, Riverside in 2019 as a music major.

      I am very passionate about music composition and production on video game music. I also took audio recording classes and put effort into learning mixing and mastering.
